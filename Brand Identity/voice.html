Voice

Lunch Money's voice is friendly, clear, and supportive. We communicate with empathy and a touch of humor to make financial topics approachable and engaging. Our tone is confident but never condescending, aiming to empower users to take control of their finances without feeling overwhelmed.

Brand Voice Overview

Lunch Money speaks with clarity, warmth, and calm confidence. The voice is designed to make personal finance feel approachable rather than intimidating. Every message from onboarding to error states should feel like it’s coming from a friendly guide who wants users to feel in control of their money. The tone avoids jargon, judgment, or pressure, focusing instead on support, simplicity, and reassurance.

Voice Principles and Examples

1. Simple, not simplistic
We explain things clearly without talking down to the user. We avoid jargon and keep sentences short, direct, and easy to understand.

❌ Don’t: “Your financial overview contains multiple categorical discrepancies that require reconciliation.”
✅ Do: “Some of your categories don’t match. Here’s how to fix them.”


2. Supportive, not preachy
We guide users without blaming them for mistakes or making them feel judged. The tone is encouraging, especially when dealing with sensitive financial topics.

❌ Don’t: “You entered this wrong. You need to be more careful.”
✅ Do: “Hmm, that doesn’t look right. Try entering a valid amount.”


3. Confident, not aggressive
We speak with clarity and purpose, but never with force. The voice is steady and trustworthy, not pushy or demanding.

❌ Don’t: “You must update your budget immediately.”
✅ Do: “It’s a good time to review your budget. Here’s what needs your attention.”


4. Warm, not overly casual
We sound human and friendly, but still professional. We avoid slang or humor that could feel out of place in a financial context.

❌ Don’t: “Oops! Your budget totally freaked out.”
✅ Do: “Looks like something went wrong. Let’s get it sorted.”


5. Encouraging progress, not perfection
We celebrate small wins and help users move forward without pressure. The tone reinforces that financial management is a journey.

❌ Don’t: “You didn’t meet your savings goal this month.”
✅ Do: “You got close to your savings goal. Let’s adjust things for next month.”
